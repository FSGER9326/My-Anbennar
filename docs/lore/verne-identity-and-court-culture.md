# Verne Identity and Court Culture

**Status:** WORKING_LORE

This file is a compact thematic reference for naming, event flavor, and mission text consistency.

## Identity baseline

- Maritime-aristocratic Vernman state with strong wyvern symbolism.
- Court identity tied to ducal/wyvern-king continuity and prestige display.
- "Great adventurer" legacy exists alongside dynastic court identity.

Repo/design anchors:

- `history/countries/A33 - Verne.txt` (starting ruler house and setup)
- `common/countries/Verne.txt` (name pools, leader/ship naming themes)
- `missions/Verne_Missions.txt` (wyvern, sea, adventurer, and imperial beats)
- `docs/design/dynasty-and-court.md`

## Institutions and court culture signals

- Courtly oath and legitimacy framing (design reform/doctrine language).
- Vernissage/court spectacle identity appears repeatedly in design material.
- Mage-court and martial-court coexistence is expected in Verne design direction.

Practical writing cue: court scenes should mix ceremony, lineage, and expeditionary ambition.

## Dynastic culture guidance

### House and ruler tone

- Keep ruler language high-formal and lineage-conscious.
- Use oath, inheritance, and continuity motifs in succession text.
- Distinguish "court pragmatists" vs "glory traditionalists" in event voices where needed.

### Naming support from repo pools

Source: `common/countries/Verne.txt`.

- Male ruler names include forms like `Alvar`, `Armoc`, `Dustin`, `Tristan`, `Valerano`, `Vercan`.
- Female ruler/consort names include forms like `Ariana`, `Aldresia`, `Cecilia`, `Lorenza`, `Valenza`.
- Dynasty and leader names include Vernman and broader Anbennari-elvenized blends.

Use these pools to keep generated character text grounded in existing culture assets.

## Wyvern military fantasy guidance

- Wyvern imagery should be military-institutional, not only mythical.
- Recurrent motifs: nests, wings, crimson/bronze scales, riders, commanderies.
- Keep tone "disciplined elite force" more than "wild beast cult" unless event context demands otherwise.

## Mission/event tone guidance

Prefer mission/event prose that blends:

1. court prestige and dynastic symbolism
2. maritime ambition and overseas route-making
3. deliberate statecraft rather than random heroism

Avoid tone drift into generic pirate or generic chivalric language detached from wyvern-maritime identity.
