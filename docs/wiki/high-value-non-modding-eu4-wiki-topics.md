# High-Value EU4 Wiki Topics (Non-Modding) For Verne Work

These are **not** from the Paradox "Modding" category.

They are useful because they explain major game systems you need to model correctly before scripting.

## Priority A (most useful first)

1. **Estates**
   - Why: many Verne systems interact with estates and privileges.
2. **Government** + **Government reforms**
   - Why: your reform triplets and government mechanic wiring depend on this.
3. **Mercenaries**
   - Why: Verne order company design depends on real merc rules.
4. **Great Projects / Monuments**
   - Why: Chapterhouse + monument coupling decisions/events.
5. **Religion (Corinite context in-game behavior)**
   - Why: pressure/disaster/religion framing should match game reality.
6. **Disasters**
   - Why: scaling crisis design should align with player expectations.
7. **Army tradition / Navy tradition / Professionalism / Drill**
   - Why: balancing military rewards and order-company identity.
8. **Legitimacy / Prestige / Power Projection / Stability / Corruption**
   - Why: core pressure variables in your design docs.
9. **Trade nodes / Trade companies / Centers of trade**
   - Why: overseas/network identity and economic route missions.
10. **Institutions**
   - Why: pacing and modernization assumptions in mission/reform rewards.

## Priority B (good second wave)

- **Subjects / Personal unions / Vassals**
- **Absolutism**
- **Age objectives and abilities**
- **Mission trees (player-side behavior)**
- **Culture and accepted cultures**
- **Advisor system (cost/levels/availability)**

## How to use these pages without bloat

For each external wiki topic, store only this in your local docs:

1. one-paragraph summary in your own words
2. what design decision it affected
3. which repo files were touched because of it
4. date checked

Do **not** dump long copied text into project docs. Keep decision-oriented notes.
